# Datasheet for TRACE-3D

Following the *Datasheet for Datasets* framework (Gebru et al.). TRACE-3D is a
benchmark: it ships a **case generator** plus license-permitted derived
artifacts, not a static corpus of raw scans.

> **NOT FOR CLINICAL USE.** Research benchmark only. See `SAFETY.md`.

---

## Motivation

**For what purpose was the dataset created?**
To evaluate whether an LLM agent can convert 3D anatomy into a **safe clinical
action** (an image-guided needle trajectory) and whether its stated confidence is
**calibrated to the true clearance margin**. No prior benchmark combined 3D /
volumetric medical simulation with closed-loop agentic evaluation of an action
scored against segmentation geometry; TRACE-3D fills that niche.

**Who created it and who funds it?**
The TRACE-3D contributors, as an open research benchmark. Code is MIT licensed.

---

## Composition

**What do the instances represent?**
Clinical **episodes**. Each episode bundles: a labeled 3D volume + affine, a
derived scene graph (centroids, laterality, surface distances, adjacency,
critical-structure labels), a gold spec (history checklist, graded DDx, spatial
beliefs, safety partial-order, gold trajectory / corridor), and the rubric.

**How are instances produced?**
Two paths:

- **Synthetic (default):** procedurally generated from a **private held-out
  seed**. Lesions are synthetic and **flagged**; localization is anchored to
  **real labeled anatomical structures**. No downloads required.
- **Real (optional `real` extra):** AMOS22 / MSD CT scans segmented with
  TotalSegmentator (default 117-class task only).

**Is any information missing?**
Yes, by design. Default-resolution TotalSegmentator does **not** label many small
vessels and bowel loops. The scene graph — and therefore the safety metric — is
**structurally blind** to structures it cannot see. See `SAFETY.md`.

**Are there labels / targets?**
Yes: gold organ / laterality / localization, gold adjacency sets, graded DDx
relevances, safety prerequisite partial-orders, and gold trajectories / optimal
safe corridors used for `corridor_regret_mm`.

**Does the data contain confidential or PII content?**
No. Only **de-identified, open** data is used. **Raw scans are never
redistributed** — only the build pipeline and license-permitted derived
artifacts. MIMIC-IV, if used at all, is **credentialed, local-only, and gated**
(never shipped).

**Are there errors or noise?**
Synthetic lesions are approximations and are flagged; DDx on synthetic cases is
treated cautiously. Segmentation is imperfect (see "missing information").

---

## Collection process

**How was the data acquired?**
Synthetic cases are generated by the shipped procedural generator. Real cases are
derived locally by the user from openly licensed public CT datasets (AMOS22, MSD)
plus TotalSegmentator; TRACE-3D ships the ingestion pipeline, not the scans.

**Over what timeframe?**
Cases are generated on demand; there is no fixed collection window. Source CT
datasets retain their own provenance and licenses (see `DATA_LICENSES.md`).

**Were people involved / consent?**
Source datasets carry their own consent/IRB provenance under their open licenses.
TRACE-3D adds no new human-subject collection.

---

## Preprocessing / cleaning / labeling

- Volumes are converted to a labeled segmentation + affine; the scene graph is
  computed in pure NumPy (centroids, laterality vs midline, surface distance
  fields, adjacency).
- Synthetic lesions are inserted and flagged.
- Gold artifacts (DDx grades, safety partial-orders, rubrics) require **>= 1
  clinician reviewer** before any publication claim (hard gate).
- Raw, un-derived scans are **not** part of any shipped artifact.

---

## Uses

**What is the dataset intended for?**
Benchmarking LLM agents on 3D-grounded, closed-loop clinical-action safety,
spatial-belief fidelity, and margin calibration.

**What should it NOT be used for?**

- **Any clinical purpose.** It must not plan, guide, or inform a real procedure.
- Training a model and then evaluating it on the same seed (contamination).
- Claims of clinical validity without clinician review of gold (hard gate).

**Contamination control.**
Cases are procedurally generated from a private held-out seed; the **generator**
ships, so fresh unseen cases can always be produced. Do not benchmark on cases
generated from a seed your model has seen.

---

## Distribution

**How is it distributed?**
As source code (MIT): the generator, scene-graph builder, orchestrator, and
scorer, plus license-permitted derived example artifacts. **Raw scans are never
distributed.** Derived scene graphs from CC BY-SA sources, if redistributed,
**propagate share-alike + attribution** (see `DATA_LICENSES.md`).

**License.**
Code: MIT. Data: per-source matrix in `DATA_LICENSES.md`.

---

## Maintenance

**Who maintains it?**
The TRACE-3D contributors.

**How can the benchmark be extended?**
By adding case templates to the generator, new anatomical targets, or new
real-CT ingestion adapters. Any new gold requires clinician review before
publication claims. Fairness invariance must be preserved: demographic-swap
variants must not change gold.

**Known limitations** (summarized; full list in `SAFETY.md`)

- Geometric proxy, not clinical endorsement: straight-line needle vs **static**
  meshes ignores respiration, tissue deformation, needle bending, contrast phase.
- Segmentation blindness to the small vessels / bowel loops that drive most
  percutaneous complications.
- Synthetic lesions are approximations (flagged).
- Clinical authority gap: gold needs clinician review before any claim.
